# INSIGHTS FROM STUDENT PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

## 1. Overall Performance

The average final grade (G3) provides a general understanding of student performance across the dataset.

## 2. Top Performing Students

The top 5 students were identified based on final grades (G3), highlighting the highest academic achievers in the dataset.

## 3. Pass vs Fail Distribution

Using a threshold of 10 marks, students were classified into pass and fail categories. This helps in understanding the success rate of students.

## 4. Performance Improvement

A group of students showed significant improvement where their final grade (G3) exceeded their initial grade (G1) by more than 5 marks. This indicates strong academic progress over time.

## 5. Performance Drop

Some students showed a decline in performance where their final grade (G3) was lower than their first exam grade (G1), indicating inconsistency or possible challenges.

## 6. Early Performance vs Final Outcome

Students with higher first exam scores (G1) generally achieved higher final grades (G3), suggesting a strong correlation between early and final performance.

## 7. Impact of Absences

Students with higher absences tended to have lower average final grades, indicating that attendance plays a key role in academic success.

## 8. Study Time Influence

Students who spent more time studying generally performed better, showing a positive relationship between study time and final grades.

## 9. Internet Access

Students with internet access showed slightly better academic performance, suggesting that access to online resources may support learning.

## 10. Overall Improvement Trend

A noticeable number of students improved from G1 to G3, indicating that many students were able to enhance their performance over time.
